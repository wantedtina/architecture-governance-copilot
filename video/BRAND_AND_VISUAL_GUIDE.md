# Brand and Visual Guide

This is a neutral, Standard Chartered-inspired visual direction for an internal hackathon PoC.
It is not an official Standard Chartered publication or proprietary template.

## Visual observations

The public materials use crisp sans-serif typography, generous whitespace, strong information
hierarchy, dark navy or white foundations, and restrained blue-to-green accents. Public pages
combine clear headings with limited decorative elements and high-contrast calls to action.

These observations inform general direction only. No template, logo, photograph, or media-library
asset is copied.

## Palette direction

| Role | Colour |
| --- | --- |
| Deep navy background | `#061D33` |
| Dark indigo | `#020B43` |
| Primary blue | `#0473EA` |
| Supporting blue | `#008ACB` |
| Primary green | `#38D200` |
| Dark green | `#238500` |
| White | `#FFFFFF` |
| Off-white surface | `#F7F8FA` |
| Charcoal copy | `#263746` |
| Muted grey | `#66727D` |

Use blue-to-green gradients only as a thin accent line, small highlight, or restrained glow.
Never use the gradient behind body copy.

## Background treatments

- Opening and closing: deep navy with a subtle blue/green edge glow.
- Process and architecture: off-white with navy headings and a thin gradient rule.
- App footage: retain the real application styling; do not recolour it in post.

## Typography

- Preferred: Aptos, Arial, or another widely available clean sans-serif.
- Deck title: 52–62 pt.
- Frame title: 36–44 pt.
- Mid-level labels: 24–28 pt.
- Body and process labels: 18–24 pt, never below 16 pt.
- Use sentence case, short phrases, and no dense paragraphs on visual frames.

## Layout and diagrams

- Use an even 16:9 grid with 80–100 px safe margins.
- Use simple process lines, restrained rounded rectangles, and clear arrows.
- Put future components on a dashed branch labelled `Future — not implemented`.
- Do not use charts or unsupported numerical comparisons.
- Keep the five frames visually related but vary composition.

## Title and closing cards

The project name is the primary element. Team and hackathon details are secondary but fully
readable. Both names and exact roles appear on opening and closing cards. Use no official logo.

## Subtitle and callout style

- Subtitle: white text, 34–40 px at 1080p, on a semi-opaque navy rounded rectangle.
- Maximum two lines where practical; maintain at least 96 px from the bottom edge.
- Callout: compact white or blue-tint label with one blue edge and no drop-shadow excess.
- Do not stack callouts over subtitles or interactive controls.

## Transitions

Use direct cuts, 6–10 frame fades, simple dissolves, and at most a subtle 103–108% crop move.
Avoid wipes, spins, dramatic motion graphics, or constant zoom.

## Logo and media restrictions

The official logo is deliberately not used. The official media-library terms restrict use of its
images and logos; no permission was confirmed for this hackathon package. Do not download or
include official logo files, photographs, campaign imagery, or proprietary templates.

If the team receives an approved internal company or event template, logo, or media asset, record
that approval and replace the neutral placeholder before final submission.

## Accessibility

- Maintain at least WCAG AA contrast for text.
- Do not rely on blue or green alone to convey implemented versus future state; include labels and
  solid versus dashed line styles.
- Keep meaningful text inside a 90 px side safe area and 96 px subtitle safe area.
- Check every exported frame at 100% size and at small player size.
